The Maker Behind Lumina

Elena Vásquez

Master Jeweler & Founder

Raised in the red-clay foothills of northern New Mexico, Elena learned silversmithing from her grandfather — a Navajo-trained metalworker who believed jewelry was the language of the land. After studying at the California College of the Arts and apprenticing under master goldsmiths in Florence, she brought her heritage and her craft back west.

In 2012, she founded Lumina in a small Santa Barbara studio, where the desert meets the sea. Every piece is handcrafted using traditional techniques, ethically sourced gemstones, and the kind of patient artisanship that machines simply cannot replicate.

"Every stone has a story. My job is to help it speak."

Jewelry Care Guide

Sterling Silver — Polish with a soft cloth after each wear. Store in an anti-tarnish pouch away from moisture and sunlight. Avoid chlorine and harsh chemicals.

Gold — Clean with warm water and a drop of dish soap. Pat dry with a soft cloth. Remove before swimming or exercising. Gold is resilient but scratch-prone.

Turquoise — Never immerse in water or expose to chemicals. Wipe gently with a dry cloth. Turquoise is porous and can absorb oils, so apply perfume before putting on jewelry.

Sea Glass & Coastal Gems — Store separately to prevent scratching. Wipe clean with a slightly damp cloth. Avoid prolonged sun exposure which can fade color.

General Tips

  • Apply perfume and makeup before wearing jewelry.
  • Remove jewelry before sleeping, showering, or swimming.
  • Store each piece separately in a soft pouch or lined box.
  • Bring pieces back to us annually for professional cleaning.
